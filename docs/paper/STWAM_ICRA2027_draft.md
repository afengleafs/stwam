## Abstract

World-action models (WAMs) couple video prediction with action generation, but recent systems rely on multi-billion-parameter video backbones and large-scale embodied pretraining. We present **STWAM**, a parameter-efficient WAM that operates entirely in a *semantic* latent space: a frozen V-JEPA 2.1 encoder with a frozen S-VAE adapter compresses observations into 96-dimensional latents, a pretrained 73.5M-parameter latent-video DiT predicts future latents, and a lightweight action expert (28.1M parameters trained from scratch, together with zero-initialized per-layer Mixture-of-Transformers adapters) reads the clean world-model context to denoise action chunks via flow matching. At inference, the video tower runs exactly once per control step; all flow-matching steps reuse cached per-layer key/value features, so action prediction does not require video rollout. With 0.44B total parameters (0.10B trainable) and no embodied pretraining, STWAM reaches **89.75%** average success on the standard LIBERO benchmark, surpassing the fine-tuned 7B OpenVLA baseline by 13.3 points and approaching fine-tuned pi0. A controlled ablation that swaps only the latent interface for a reconstruction-oriented SD3-VAE (V-TWAM) drops average success to **69.25%** (−20.5), with the largest losses on object-centric (−31) and long-horizon (−35) suites — extending recent world-model-level evidence for semantic latents to closed-loop policy success. A full five-axis LIBERO-PRO evaluation shows STWAM is nearly invariant to language rewrites (89.0% vs. 89.8% original) but fails under spatial rearrangement and task redefinition, delimiting what semantic latents do and do not buy. Code and evaluation logs will be released.

**Keywords:** world models, vision-language-action, representation learning for manipulation, robustness evaluation

## I. Introduction

Vision-language-action (VLA) policies and world-action models (WAMs) have rapidly improved language-conditioned manipulation, with recent systems reporting >97% average success on the LIBERO benchmark [OpenVLA-OFT, Fast-WAM, Cosmos-Policy, LingBot-VA]. This progress, however, is coupled to scale: state-of-the-art WAMs build on 5B–8B video-generative backbones (e.g., Wan2.2 in Fast-WAM, Cosmos in Cosmos Policy) and, in the VLA case, large-scale embodied pretraining. Two questions remain under-examined. First, *how much of the WAM recipe survives at small scale* — can the video-co-training benefit be realized with a sub-100M trainable budget on consumer GPUs? Second, *what should the world model predict* — recent controlled evidence on robotic world models [Semantic-WM] suggests that latent spaces aligned with pretrained semantic encoders (V-JEPA 2, DINO, SigLIP) preserve action- and task-relevant structure better than reconstruction-oriented VAE latents, but this evidence was established for world-model evaluation (planning probes, policy-in-the-loop rollouts) on BridgeV2, not for the closed-loop success of a policy *trained inside* such a latent space.

This paper addresses both questions with **STWAM** (Semantic-laTent World-Action Model). STWAM freezes a V-JEPA 2.1 ViT-L encoder and its S-VAE adapter, so all world modeling and action generation happen in a compact 96-d semantic latent space. A pretrained latent-video DiT-S (73.5M) serves as the video expert; it is trained with v-prediction diffusion forcing on future latents while the observed history frames are always teacher-forced clean, so the context the action expert reads at training time matches inference exactly. A lightweight action expert is coupled to the video tower through per-layer, zero-initialized joint Mixture-of-Transformers (MoT) adapters with a time-aligned rotary embedding that maps action-chunk positions onto the video frame axis. Actions are generated by flow matching; crucially, the video tower is *not* action-conditioned, so at inference it is prefilled once per control step and all flow steps run only the small action expert against cached key/value features — video co-training at training time, direct action prediction at test time, in the spirit of Fast-WAM but two orders of magnitude smaller in trainable parameters.

We evaluate STWAM on the four standard LIBERO suites and on the full five-perturbation LIBERO-PRO protocol, and we run a controlled latent-interface ablation, **V-TWAM**, that keeps the entire WAM/action/adapter pathway fixed and replaces only the semantic latent with a 16-d SD3-VAE reconstruction latent. Our findings:

1. **Parameter efficiency.** With 0.44B total / 0.10B trainable parameters, no embodied pretraining, and training on 4×RTX 5090 GPUs, STWAM reaches 89.75% average on standard LIBERO — +13.3 points over fine-tuned OpenVLA (7B) and within 4.4 points of fine-tuned pi0 (~3B), though 7–9 points below the best 2026 WAMs.
2. **Semantic latents matter for policy success, not just world-model probes.** V-TWAM drops to 69.25% average (−20.5), with the losses concentrated exactly where semantic structure should help: LIBERO-Object (98.0→67.0) and LIBERO-Long (83.0→48.0). Under LIBERO-PRO perturbations the gap persists (37.65% vs. 25.15% full-protocol mean). This is, to our knowledge, the first closed-loop policy-level confirmation of the semantic-vs-reconstruction latent hypothesis of [Semantic-WM].
3. **A calibrated robustness picture.** STWAM is nearly invariant to instruction rewrites (Semantic perturbation: 89.0% vs. 89.8% original) yet collapses under object rearrangement (Position: 0%) and task redefinition (Task: 4.5%). We analyze these failures and argue they delimit the semantic-latent design itself: the 96-d compressed latent preserves object and language semantics but not fine-grained spatial layout or contact geometry.

## II. Related Work

### A. Vision-language-action models

Large VLA policies fine-tune vision-language backbones for action prediction: OpenVLA (7B) [OpenVLA], its optimized fine-tuning recipe OpenVLA-OFT, and the pi0 / pi0.5 family combine internet-scale VLM pretraining with large robot datasets. VLA-JEPA shows that JEPA-style predictive visual representations transfer well to control without robot pretraining, reporting 97.2% LIBERO average. STWAM shares VLA-JEPA's representation choice (V-JEPA) but differs in mechanism: rather than conditioning a policy on encoder features, STWAM trains a *predictive world model* in the JEPA latent space and couples an action expert to it.

### B. World-action models and video co-training

WAMs couple video generation with action decoding. Fast-WAM demonstrates that co-training a video diffusion backbone with an action head improves the policy even when, at inference, only direct action prediction is used; Cosmos Policy and LingBot-VA reach 98.5% LIBERO average with 5B–8B backbones. Motus reports 97.6% on LIBERO-Long. All of these operate in reconstruction-oriented pixel/VAE latent spaces of large pretrained video generators. STWAM asks whether the same co-training principle holds when (i) the world model is a 73.5M DiT-S and (ii) the latent space is semantic rather than reconstructive. Our inference-time scheme — prefill the video tower once, run flow-matching steps only through the light action expert — follows Fast-WAM's "co-train with video, act directly" recipe.

### C. Reconstruction vs. semantic latents for robotic world models

Semantic-WM [arXiv:2605.06388] fixes the transition model, data, and training protocol on BridgeV2 and varies only the latent interface, finding that semantic encoders (V-JEPA 2.1, Web-DINO, SigLIP 2) outperform SD3-VAE/Cosmos reconstruction latents on action recoverability, CEM planning, VLA-in-the-loop success, and OOD robustness (+9.8 pts VLA SR, family-level bootstrap p=0.013), while an S-VAE adapter to 96-d eases diffusion at some cost in fine action geometry. STWAM adopts exactly this V-JEPA 2.1 + S-VAE 96-d interface and inherits its pretrained DiT-S. Our contribution relative to Semantic-WM is orthogonal and confirmatory: we move from world-model probes to *closed-loop policy success* in a different domain (LIBERO simulation vs. BridgeV2 real-robot video), with an in-house action expert instead of an external OpenVLA rolled out inside the model, and we report where the hypothesis breaks (spatial rearrangement, task redefinition).

### D. Robustness evaluation for LIBERO

LIBERO-PRO [arXiv:2510.03827] perturbs the standard suites along five axes — Object, Position, Semantic (instruction rewrites), Task, and Environment — to test whether high LIBERO scores reflect generalizable competence or memorization of initial states and phrasings. Published leaderboard results show that even strong VLAs collapse on Position (OpenVLA and pi0: 0%) and Task (0–6.8%). We report, to our knowledge, the first complete five-axis LIBERO-PRO matrix for a WAM-style policy and use it as a diagnostic rather than a headline number.

## III. Method

Fig. 1 gives an overview. `[TODO: 总览图 — 左: frozen V-JEPA+S-VAE encoder; 中: video DiT tower (teacher-forced clean history + noisy future); 右: action expert + per-layer MoT adapters; 推理路径高亮 "prefill once, cached K/V". 可基于 docs/paper/figures/mot_adapter_arch.html 重绘]`

### A. Problem setup

At each control step the policy observes two RGB views (workspace and wrist camera), proprioceptive state $s_t \in \mathbb{R}^{8}$, and a language instruction $\ell$, and must output an action chunk $a_{t:t+K} \in \mathbb{R}^{K\times 7}$ with $K{=}32$, all of which is executed before re-planning.

### B. Semantic latent interface (frozen)

Following the adapter-96 recipe of Semantic-WM, each $256\times256$ frame is encoded by a frozen V-JEPA 2.1 ViT-L (342.8M parameters) and a frozen S-VAE transformer adapter into a latent grid $z_t \in \mathbb{R}^{16\times16\times96}$; the two camera views are concatenated along width, giving $[T,16,32,96]$ per clip. The S-VAE was trained with feature-MSE, cosine, spectral, and KL objectives (pixel losses do not back-propagate into the adapter), so the latent preserves semantic feature geometry rather than pixel detail. No component of this interface is updated during STWAM training.

### C. Video expert: latent diffusion forcing with clean history

The video expert is the semantic-wm DiT-S (dim 384, 12 layers, 6 heads, wide DDT head, 73.5M parameters), initialized from the released `DiT-S_D96` checkpoint and fine-tuned. It denoises future latents under v-prediction with a per-frame diffusion-forcing schedule and a dimension-aware time-distribution shift (2.45) appropriate for non-VAE latents. Two design choices matter for the policy coupling:

- **Teacher-forced clean history.** The first $H{=}1$ frame of every training clip is always assigned $t{=}0$ and kept as an exact latent; the video loss is computed on future frames only. Consequently, the features the action expert reads during training are *identical in distribution* to what it reads at inference (where only clean context frames exist) — there is no train/test mismatch from noisy context.
- **No action conditioning of the video tower.** Language and proprioception enter through cross-attention context; actions do not. This decouples the video tower from the action sampler and is what enables single-prefill inference (Sec. III-E).

### D. Action expert and joint MoT adapters

The action expert is a 12-layer transformer with hidden size 128 (4 heads), trained from scratch, that denoises the action chunk under conditional flow matching: given corrupted chunk $x_\tau = (1{-}\tau) a + \tau \epsilon$, it predicts the velocity $\epsilon - a$.

Video and action towers are coupled by **per-layer joint MoT adapters** (inner dim 384, zero-initialized outputs), one per DiT layer. At layer $L$, the adapter (i) lets the action tokens cross-attend to the video features of the *clean history frames only*, and (ii) applies a gated language/state update to the video features. Because all adapter outputs are zero-initialized, training starts from the intact pretrained video expert and an independent action expert, and the coupling grows only as far as it reduces the loss. A time-aligned rotary embedding places the $K$ action-chunk positions onto the video frame-time axis, spanning from the last observed frame to the end of the predicted window, so cross-attention is aware of *when* within the predicted future each action step sits.

The trainable budget is: DiT-S video expert 73.5M (fine-tuned) + action expert, MoT adapters, and proprio encoder 28.1M (from scratch) = 101.6M trainable of 444M total.

### E. Training objective and inference

Training minimizes $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{video}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{action}}$ with unit weights, where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{video}}$ is the v-prediction MSE on future frames and $\mathcal{L}_{\text{action}}$ the flow-matching MSE over the chunk (pad-masked). Text is encoded by frozen Flan-T5-Large embeddings (precomputed).

At inference the video tower runs **exactly once** per control step: the clean context latents are prefilled through the 12 DiT blocks, and each adapter's key/value projections of the post-block features are cached. Each of the 10 flow-matching steps then runs only the 128-d action expert against this cache — the per-step cost is that of a 12-layer, width-128 transformer over 32 tokens. On a single RTX 5090 (bf16), the WAM towers plan a 32-step chunk in **210.6 ± 4.9 ms** (prefill-once), versus 505.1 ± 13.2 ms if the video tower were re-run at every flow step — a **2.40×** saving from the cached-K/V scheme — amortizing to **6.6 ms of planning per executed action** (V-JEPA encoding, run once per control step, is reported separately). Video generation is never needed at test time; the future-prediction objective acts purely as a training-time regularizer, consistent with Fast-WAM's finding that co-training, not test-time rollout, carries the benefit.

## IV. Experiments

We answer four questions. **Q1:** How far does a 0.1B-trainable semantic-latent WAM go on standard LIBERO? **Q2:** Does the semantic latent causally matter for policy success (controlled V-TWAM ablation)? **Q3:** What does the full LIBERO-PRO protocol reveal about *what kind* of generalization semantic latents buy? **Q4:** Which training-recipe factors matter (k-draws study)?

### A. Setup

**Benchmark.** Standard LIBERO: Spatial / Object / Goal / Long (libero-10), 10 tasks each, 10 episodes per task (100 trials per suite). LIBERO-PRO: the five official perturbations (Object, Position/Swap, Semantic, Task, Environment) applied to all four suites, 10 tasks × 10 episodes per cell — 2,000 additional trials per model.

**Training.** A single joint policy is trained on all 40 LIBERO tasks (no per-suite specialization, no embodied pretraining): 300k steps, global batch 32, 4×RTX 5090, ~146 h wall-clock, bfloat16. The same checkpoint is used for every evaluation in this paper.

**Baselines.** Published LIBERO numbers for fine-tuned OpenVLA, pi0, pi0.5, OpenVLA-OFT, VLA-JEPA, Fast-WAM, Cosmos Policy, LingBot-VA, Motus (sources cited per row); published LIBERO-PRO leaderboard numbers for OpenVLA, pi0, pi0.5, MolmoAct, NORA, x-VLA. Cross-paper numbers use each paper's own evaluation protocol; we therefore avoid over-reading differences below ~2 points.

**V-TWAM (controlled ablation).** Identical WAM/action/MoT pathway, identical training recipe and 300k budget; only the latent interface is replaced by the SD3 VAE ($16\times$ downsampling, 16 channels, patch size 2). This isolates the latent space as the single varied factor, mirroring the controlled design of Semantic-WM at the policy level.

### C. Q2: Semantic vs. reconstruction latent — the V-TWAM ablation (Table II)

**Table II — Controlled latent-interface ablation (same architecture, recipe, and budget).**

| Suite | STWAM (V-JEPA/S-VAE 96-d) | V-TWAM (SD3-VAE 16-d) | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Spatial | 88.0 | 81.0 | −7.0 |
| Object | 98.0 | 67.0 | −31.0 |

| **Average** | **89.75** | **69.25** | **−20.5** |
| LIBERO-PRO full mean | 37.65 | 25.15 | −12.5 |

The pattern, not just the magnitude, is the finding. The VAE latent supports a serviceable policy on suites dominated by short-horizon motion toward salient targets (Spatial −7, Goal −9) but collapses precisely where the world model must bind object identity to affordances (Object −31) and maintain task structure across long horizons (Long −35). Under LIBERO-PRO, V-TWAM also loses most on Object perturbations (65.3→38.8 mean) and Semantic rewrites (89.0→71.2). This is the closed-loop analogue of Semantic-WM's world-model-level result — reconstruction latents "look right" but under-encode action- and task-relevant state — and it transfers across domain (BridgeV2→LIBERO), objective (world-model probes→policy success), and action head (external OpenVLA→our flow-matching expert). Notably, both models score 0 on Position/Swap and ≤4.5 on Task: switching to a semantic latent does not touch these axes at all, foreshadowing Sec. IV-D.

**Mechanism probe: action recoverability of the frozen latents.** To rule out "the VAE policy merely trained worse," we probe the *encoders themselves*, independent of any policy: for latent pairs $(z_i, z_{i+k})$ from LIBERO clips (spatial mean+std pooling), a small MLP is trained to regress the intervening action segment, and we report test-set Pearson $r$ averaged over the 7 action dimensions (7,680 pairs at $k{=}1$; 4,800 at $k{=}4$; identical probe capacity and data for both encoders). The V-JEPA/S-VAE latent yields $r = \mathbf{0.892}/\mathbf{0.878}$ ($k{=}1/4$) versus $0.799/0.817$ for the SD3-VAE latent. The semantic latent thus exposes action-induced state change more linearly *before any policy is trained on it* — the same ordering, at the policy-data level, as Semantic-WM's IDM analysis (their Table 2) on BridgeV2. The success-rate gap in Table II is therefore not an optimization accident but a property of the latent interface.

Three observations, stated deliberately as diagnosis rather than advertisement:

1. **Language robustness is a genuine strength.** Under instruction rewrites STWAM retains 89.0% vs. 89.8% original — comparable to pi0 (90.5) and above MolmoAct/NORA — supporting the claim that a language-conditioned policy over V-JEPA semantic latents binds behavior to meaning rather than to surface phrasing.
2. **Object perturbation is mixed and structured.** Spatial/Object suites hold at 88%, so the model is not merely memorizing appearances; but Goal/Object and Long/Object fall to 44/41%, indicating that affordance binding for *task-critical* objects and fixtures in longer tasks still depends on the training distribution.
3. **Position (0%) and Task (4.5%) collapse, for STWAM and V-TWAM alike — and for OpenVLA and pi0 on the public leaderboard.** The failure is therefore not specific to semantic latents, but semantic latents also do nothing to fix it. We attribute this to two compounding factors: (i) the S-VAE 96-d compression is known to sacrifice fine action geometry (Semantic-WM's adapter trade-off), and (ii) nothing in the training objective forces the policy to re-ground object *placement* or re-read the task goal when the scene contradicts the memorized initial layout. STWAM's full-protocol mean (37.65) is below the 40–53 leaderboard range of large VLAs; we present this gap, and the 89.75→37.65 drop from standard LIBERO, as the honest generalization boundary of the current system.

## V. Limitations

(i) All results are in LIBERO simulation with a single embodiment; no real-robot validation yet. (ii) STWAM trails 2026 SOTA WAMs by 7–9 points on standard LIBERO, and our efficiency claim concerns parameters/hardware, not training steps (300k vs. Fast-WAM's reported 20k). (iii) The V-TWAM comparison varies the latent interface under one matched budget; we did not sweep VAE-side capacity (e.g., larger DiT for the VAE latent), which Semantic-WM found narrows *visual* but not action-centric gaps. (iv) The Position/Task collapse means the current system should not be deployed where scene layout or goals deviate from the training distribution; addressing it — higher-dimensional or native semantic latents, explicit pose/depth tokens, layout-randomized data — is the clearest next step. (v) Encoders differ in pretraining data and capacity; as in Semantic-WM, we cannot fully separate "semantic objective" from "pretraining scale" as the cause of the semantic-latent advantage.

## VI. Conclusion

STWAM shows that the world-action-model recipe — co-train a predictive video model with an action expert, act directly at inference — survives aggressive scale reduction when the world model lives in a frozen semantic latent space: 0.10B trainable parameters, consumer GPUs, no embodied pretraining, 89.75% standard LIBERO. Its controlled V-TWAM ablation provides the first closed-loop, policy-level confirmation that semantic latents beat reconstruction latents for manipulation, precisely on object-affordance and long-horizon structure. Its complete LIBERO-PRO matrix shows with equal clarity what semantic latents do not provide: spatial-rearrangement and task-redefinition robustness. We offer STWAM both as a practical low-budget WAM and as a calibrated data point on where the field's current latent-space assumptions succeed and fail.
